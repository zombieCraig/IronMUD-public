# Weapon Damage Balance Reference

This guide provides damage dice recommendations for weapons across different settings. Use these tables when creating weapons to keep combat balanced.

## Design Principles

### Anchor Points

Balance is built around these known reference points:

| Reference | Dice | Avg Damage |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Unarmed (fists) | 1d2 | 1.5 |
| Level 1 mobile | 1d4 | 2.5 |
| Level 2 mobile | 1d6 | 3.5 |
| Level 4 mobile | 1d8+1 | 5.5 |
| Level 6 mobile | 2d6 | 7.0 |
| Level 8 mobile | 2d8+2 | 11.0 |
| Level 10 mobile | 3d8+4 | 17.5 |
| Player HP | — | 100 |

### Time-to-Kill Targets

At a ~65% hit rate, a fair duel between equally geared players should last roughly 25-35 rounds. Solo combat against a level-appropriate mob should feel challenging but survivable.

- **Light weapons** (1d4–1d6): Many hits needed, rewards sustained fighting
- **Standard weapons** (1d8–1d10): Solid damage, the workhorse tier
- **Heavy weapons** (2d6–2d8): Strong hits, often two-handed
- **Legendary/artifact** (2d10+): Reserved for rare quest rewards

### Ranged Weapon Multiplier Effect

Ranged weapons with burst or auto fire modes deal their per-shot damage multiple times per round:

- **Single fire**: 1 shot per round (full per-shot damage applies once)
- **Burst fire**: 3 shots per round (per-shot damage x3)
- **Auto fire**: All loaded rounds per round (per-shot damage x magazine)

Because of this, **per-shot damage for burst/auto weapons must be lower** than melee equivalents. A 1d8 assault rifle on burst deals an effective 3d8 (avg 13.5) per round — already heavier than a greatsword.

### Effective DPS Examples (Modern Ranged)

These show how fire mode multiplies per-shot damage:

| Weapon | Per-Shot | Fire Mode | Effective Per-Round | Avg |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| 9mm Pistol | 1d8 | single | 1d8 | 4.5 |
| 9mm Pistol | 1d8 | burst | 3d8 | 13.5 |
| Assault Rifle | 1d8 | single | 1d8 | 4.5 |
| Assault Rifle | 1d8 | burst | 3d8 | 13.5 |
| Assault Rifle | 1d8 | auto (30) | 30d8 | 135 |
| SMG | 1d6 | burst | 3d6 | 10.5 |
| SMG | 1d6 | auto (30) | 30d6 | 105 |

**Note:** Auto fire empties the entire magazine in one round. Accuracy decreases with each shot and ammo is consumed rapidly, so effective damage is significantly lower than the theoretical maximum.

## Mobile Level Cross-Reference

Use this table to match weapon damage to the mob levels they're intended to fight:

| Mob Level | Mob Damage | Appropriate Player Weapon | Dice |
|-----------|------------|---------------------------|------|
| 1 | 1d4 | Light (knife, club) | 1d4 |
| 2 | 1d6 | Standard light (shortsword, mace) | 1d6 |
| 3-4 | 1d8+1 | Standard martial (longsword, pistol) | 1d8 |
| 5-6 | 2d6 | Heavy (bastard sword, rifle per-shot) | 1d10 |
| 7-8 | 2d8+2 | Two-handed power (greatsword, shotgun) | 2d6 |
| 9-10 | 3d8+4 | Elite (sniper rifle, monofilament) | 2d8 |

Players should always deal somewhat less damage per hit than the mob they're fighting, since players have 100 HP and healing options while mobs have level-scaled HP pools.
